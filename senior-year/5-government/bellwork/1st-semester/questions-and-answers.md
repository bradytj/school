1. What form of government do we have in the U.S.?
    - Federal republic or representative democracy
2. How many branches of government make up our federal government?
    - Three branches
3. The present day U.S. was originally a colony of ____________________________.
    - Great Britain/England
4. List the four characteristics of a state.
    - Population, territory, sovereignty, government
5. According to John Locke, what is the purpose of government.
    - Protect natural rights (life, liberty, property)
6. The legal system of this type of government is base on the country's religion.
    - Theocracy
7. Everything a government does is considered ________________________________.
    - Public policy
8. List the four factors of the Free Enterprise system.
    - Private ownership, profit motive, competition, consumer sovereignty
9. ___________________________ is the idea that a government only exists with the consent of the governed.
    - Popular sovereignty
10. List the five basic concepts of American democracy.
    - Worth of the individual, equality of all persons, majority rule/minority rights, necessity of compromise, individual freedom
11. The supreme law of the land is the ______________________.
    - Constitution
12. List the three basic concepts of government brought to North America by the English.
    - Ordered government, limited government, representative government
13. Name three English documents that influenced american policial ideas.
    - Magna Carta, Petition of Right, English Bill of Rights
14. This English document challenged the divine right of kings, declaring even a monarch must obey the law.
    - Magna Carta
15. This English document proclaimed the power of a Monarch was not absolute.
    - Petition of Right
16. Explain "taxation with representation."
    - Colonists could only be taxed by their own representatives
17. What was the Albany Plan of Union?
    - Benjamin Franklin's plan for colonial unity/cooperation
18. This marked the first time a significant number of colonies had joined together to oppose the British government.
    - First Continental Congress
19. What happened on April 19th, 1775?
    - Battles of Lexington and Concord (start of Revolutionary War)
20. List the five features of state constitutions.
    - Popular sovereignty, limited government, civil rights/liberties, separation of powers, checks and balances
21. What is the Mian point of Thomas Paine's pamphlet Common Sense?
    - Argued for American independence from Britain
22. The United States' first form of government was the _____________________________.
    - Articles of Confederation
23. The Articles of Confederation failed because _____________________________________.
    - Weak central government, no power to tax, no executive branch
24. What year was the United States Constitution written?
    - 1787
25. Who is the "Father of the Constitution?"
    - James Madison
26. This document became the model for the new United States Constitution.
    - Virginia Plan
27. A series of essays written to support the new constitution.
    - The Federalist Papers
28. What was the Connecticut Compromise?
    - Created bicameral legislature (Great Compromise)
29. What are the three parts of the constitution?
    - Preamble, Articles, Amendments
30. How many articles make up the constitution?
    - Seven articles
31. What are the first three articles of the constitution about?
    - Legislative, Executive, and Judicial branches
32. Explain the Supremacy Clause.
    - Constitution is supreme law; federal law overrides state law when in conflict
33. List the 6 principles of government.
    - Popular sovereignty, limited government, separation of powers, checks and balances, judicial review, federalism
34. How many times has the constitution been amended?
    - 27 times
35. The process of constitutional change has come about in what two basic ways?
    - Formal amendment and informal methods (interpretation, custom, etc.)
36. How does the Chief Executive influence the amendment process?
    - No formal role (can advocate but doesn't ratify)
37. The division of governmental power is called_____________________________.
    - Federalism
38. List the three types of Federal powers.
    - Delegated/enumerated, implied, inherent
39. Why is the Supremacy Clause called the "linchpin of the Constitution?"
    - Holds federal system together by establishing federal law as supreme
40. List the three types of delegated powers.
    - Expressed, implied, inherent
41. Give an example of reasonable and unreasonable distinction.
    - Reasonable: age for voting; Unreasonable: race-based distinctions
42. The powers the constitution gives the states are called ____________________________.
    - Reserved powers
43. List some examples of reasonable distinction allowed by the Privileges and Immunities Clause.
    - Residency requirements for voting, in-state tuition, professional licenses
44. What is the term of a U.S. Senator?
    - Six years
45. How long is a term of Congress?
    - Two years
46. How many sessions make up a term of congress?
    - Two sessions
47. How often are the seats in the House of Representatives reapportioned?
    - Every 10 years (after census)
48. List the qualifications for a U.S. Senator.
    - 30 years old, citizen for 9 years, resident of state
49. List the qualifications for the House of Representatives.
    - 25 years old, citizen for 7 years, resident of state
50. The constitutional basis for the existence of implied powers is found in the _____&_____ clause.
    - Necessary and Proper clause (Elastic clause)
51. The act of lying under oath is called _________________________.
    - Perjury
52. Strict constructionists were led by ________________ and said that congress could only exercise what two types of powers?
    - Thomas Jefferson; expressed and implied powers
53. The _________________________ is the leader of the House of Representatives.
    - Speaker of the House
54. The ______________________ presides over the Senate.
    - Vice President
55. What is a pocket veto?
    - President doesn't sign bill and Congress adjourns within 10 days
56. What is a line - item veto?
    - Power to veto specific parts of a bill (ruled unconstitutional)
57. An unpopular provision added to an important bill certain to pass is called a ___________________________?
    - Rider
58. If the President stands trial for impeachment, in which house of congress is the trial conducted?
    - Senate
59. If a president is convicted of impeachment charges, what is his punishment?
    - Removal from office
60. What two executive powers does the Constitution give to the Senate?
    - Approve treaties and confirm appointments
